# Capital Radar - Market Headquarters Product Brief

## Vision

Capital Radar should become Jun's market headquarters: a web-based command surface that automates market information, turns global macro and institutional signals into structured metadata, visualizes how forces move through the portfolio, and produces disciplined investment blueprints.

The ambition is not to predict markets with false certainty. The ambition is to build a repeatable intelligence and decision system that improves capital allocation through source discipline, risk visibility, opportunity scouting, and explicit decision triggers.

## Core product promise

Capital Radar helps answer every day:

1. What is the market regime?
2. What global forces are changing?
3. Which holdings are helped, harmed, crowded, or misunderstood?
4. Which future holdings deserve research?
5. Where is portfolio risk hidden?
6. What evidence would upgrade, downgrade, trim, exit, or investigate a position?
7. What should be watched next?

## Market headquarters modules

### 1. Command Dashboard

A one-page executive view:

- Market posture
- Risk level
- Strongest holding
- Weakest holding
- Highest-risk position
- Rebalance pressure
- Top market force
- Top opportunity theme
- Watch triggers

### 2. Market Force Map

Metadata layer that translates raw data into strategic forces:

- Rates pressure
- Risk appetite
- AI infrastructure
- Crypto liquidity beta
- Credit conditions
- Inflation expectations
- Consumer pressure
- Dollar liquidity
- Regulatory/geopolitical risk

Each force has:

- Direction
- Intensity 0-5
- Affected holdings
- Beneficiaries
- Vulnerable exposures
- Evidence
- Confidence
- Time horizon

### 3. Portfolio Exposure Map

Shows what the portfolio really owns:

- Index anchor
- Core compounders
- AI/infrastructure compounders
- Levered/decay products
- Speculative thesis-verification holdings
- Crypto/proxy exposure
- Rate-sensitive growth

### 4. Holdings Strategy Surface

Every holding becomes a decision card:

- Price and recent movement
- Portfolio weight
- Health score
- Computed action signal
- Role
- Thesis
- Bear case
- Add/trim/exit trigger
- Evidence links

### 5. Opportunity Scout

Future holdings are not random ticker ideas. They are research queues generated from active forces:

- AI infrastructure picks-and-shovels
- Quality compounders after pullback
- Defensive quality if credit/risk weakens
- Cleaner substitutes for levered exposure
- Consumer resilience names

Each gets a blueprint score and research signal.

### 6. Global Institution Intelligence

Capital Radar should ingest and summarize global institutional reports:

- FRED / Federal Reserve
- BIS
- IMF
- World Bank
- US Treasury Fiscal Data
- OECD
- ECB / BOJ / PBOC / major central banks
- JPMorgan Guide to the Markets
- BlackRock Investment Institute
- Vanguard / Fidelity / Schwab outlooks
- SEC filings and company reports

Extraction target:

- Key thesis
- Asset allocation implication
- Macro regime call
- Relevant charts/data
- Risks and counterevidence
- Geography / asset class
- Publication date
- Confidence and source quality

### 7. Risk Committee

The dashboard should always include adversarial thinking:

- What can go wrong?
- What is crowded?
- What is path-dependent?
- What is correlated?
- What looks good only because liquidity is easy?
- What requires human review?

## Guardrails

Capital Radar should avoid:

- Buy/sell language
- Overconfident predictions
- Fake precision
- Raw news overload
- Decorative charts without decision use
- Automatic trading
- Strategy claims without source/evidence

The system should be a disciplined analyst and risk committee, not a hype cockpit.
